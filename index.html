Frequently Asked Questions

A few questions we hear from small business owners all the time when they're trying to figure out where to start.

Where should a small business owner actually start?

Honestly, start with the website. If your site is slow, hard to read, or doesn't rank for the services you sell, SEO and paid ads will just pour money into a leaky bucket. Get a quick, honest look at your site's speed, structure, and conversion first. Everything else builds on top of that.

Is SEO or paid ads better for a small budget?

Paid ads work faster but stop the minute you switch them off. SEO takes longer, but it keeps paying you back for years. Most small businesses that do well run a small paid program for quick leads while slowly building up SEO in the background as a long-term asset.

Do I really need a custom website, or is a template fine?

Templates are totally fine when you're just starting out and testing an idea. Once your business has a clear offer, regular customers, and more than a handful of pages, templates usually start getting in your way. At that point, a proper custom build tends to be cheaper over time than constantly patching a template.

How long before I actually see results?

It depends on the work. Speed and technical fixes can show wins in a couple of weeks. Paid ads can bring in leads within days once tracking is working. SEO and content usually take 3 to 6 months to really show up, and about a year before they become a reliable source of business.

What's the biggest mistake you see small businesses make?

Running every channel as its own little island. A website built without SEO in mind, ads pointing at pages nobody tuned, analytics nobody trusts. Each one is fine on its own, but they never add up to anything. The whole point of this page is to help you think about these things as one system.
